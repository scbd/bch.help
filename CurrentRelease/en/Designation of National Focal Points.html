Designation of National Focal Points
BCH Central Portal Online Help > BCH Help Complete Manuals > Information-sharing Obligations On Becoming a Party to the Protocol > On Entry Into Force of the Protocol > Designation of National Focal Points

Glossary Item Box

 

Article 19 of the Protocol requires each Party to designate one national focal point for the Protocol.

The national focal point is the primary contact point between a Party and the Secretariat. This is the person or national institution that receives, for example:

 

  • Notifications from the Secretariat, regarding, for example, newly available resources or requests to designate delegates for meetings;
  • Invitations to submit views on matters relating to the Protocol; and
  • Documentation for Biosafety meetings.

 

The name and address of the focal point must be provided to the Secretariat no later than the date of entry into force of the Protocol for that Party. The nomination of the focal point must be submitted in writing either by a responsible Ministry (for example, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs), or by an existing focal point for the Convention on Biological Diversity.

In accordance with standard procedure for nomination of all focal points, written confirmation of the nomination of a person or institution to the post of any category of National Focal Point must be sent to the CBD Secretariat before such records can be created (i.e. they are the only type of national record that cannot be created directly through the Management Centre of the BCH).

Written confirmation may either be sent by fax or postal mail to the Secretariat on Ministry letterhead, signed by a Focal Point for the Protocol or Convention, or the responsible Minister. Email confirmation may be used, as long as the email originates from an account that has previously been validated by the Secretariat (i.e. an existing focal point).

The information that should be provided to register a National Focal Point is contained in the National Focal Point and Contact Details Common Formats available at http://bch.biodiv.org/resources/commonformats.shtml.
