Facilitating a network of partners

Glossary Item Box

BCH focal points play an important role in facilitating the development of a network of BCH users at a national (and/or regional) level. In order for this network to aid in the implementation process of the BCH, they typically come from a variety of institutions, sectors and disciplines, in order to address the needs of all relevant players at national level.

One of the activities that may be carried out by the BCH Focal Point in this regard is to advise the relevant national authorities when relevant information is made available through the BCH (e.g. when another Party registers a decision to place on the market an LMO intended for direct use as a food). Accordingly, all BCH focal points are automatically subscribed to the BCH Current Awareness Service. This service sends out regular updates summarizing new information that has been added to the BCH. It is sent directly to users by email or to a specified fax number (See Module 3 for further details.)

There are also several resources available in the BCH to assist focal points to network and share information more effectively. For example, the Directory of Biosafety Organizations provides assistance in developing relationships with foreign organizations with similar goals. Also, the Biosafety Information Resource Centre (BIRC), Capacity-Building databases and online conferences provide greater access to information, training, experience and fundraising opportunities.
