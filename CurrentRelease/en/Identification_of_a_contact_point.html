Identification of a contact point for unintentional transboundary movements and emergency measures
BCH Central Portal Online Help > Information-sharing Obligations On Becoming a Party to the Protocol > On Entry Into Force of the Protocol > Identification of a contact point for unintentional transboundary movements and emergency measures

Glossary Item Box

Article 17 of the Protocol requires each Party to make available to the BCH, no later than the date of entry into force of the Protocol, details of its point of contact for the purpose of receiving notifications concerning any occurrence that leads or may lead to unintentional transboundary movement of an LMO that is likely to have significant adverse effects on the conservation and sustainable use of biological diversity, taking also into account risks to human health in such States.

Because an incident of this nature may happen at any time, countries need to be prepared for emergency measures and make their contact point’s details available through the BCH.

The information that should be provided to register the contact point for unintentional transboundary movements and emergency measures is contained in the National Focal Point and Contact Details Common Formats available at http://bch.biodiv.org/resources/commonformats.shtml. 

 

Checklist 1: No later than entry into force of the Protocol

r

Designate a National Focal Point for the Protocol (Article 19.1)

r

Provide the name and address of the National Focal Point to the CBD Secretariat (Article 19.2)

r

Designate one or more Competent National Authorities (Article 19.1)

r

Provide the name and address of the Competent National Authorities to the CBD Secretariat (Article 19.2)

r

In cases where more than one competent national authority has been designated, also advise the Secretariat of the responsibilities of each authority (including at a minimum, the type of LMOs that each authority is responsible for).

r

Identify an Article 17 point of contact to receive notification concerning any occurrence that may lead to unintentional transboundary movement of an LMO.

r

Provide the name and address of the Article 17 focal point to the CBD Secretariat.

r

Designate a BCH focal point in order to be able to fulfill information-sharing obligations (BCH Modalities of Operation)
